# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Engineers

### Role Summary
QA Engineers own quality validation across the full software lifecycle. They design and execute test plans, manage test automation, and act as the final gate before features are released to production.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans, test cases, and test suites
- Execute manual and automated tests (unit, integration, E2E, regression)
- Report defects clearly and track them to resolution
- Define and enforce acceptance criteria alongside Product Managers
- Collaborate with developers on testability and quality standards
- Maintain quality dashboards and communicate risk to the project team

### Goals
- Ensure every release meets defined acceptance criteria
- Minimize defect escape rate to production
- Build a reliable, fast-feedback automated test suite

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups with the delivery team
- Bug reports and defect triage sessions
- Test summary reports before each release

---

## DevOps / Release Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps/Release Engineers design, build, and maintain the CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and deployment processes that enable fast, safe, and repeatable software delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure, environments, and configuration
- Implement monitoring, alerting, and observability tooling
- Coordinate releases with the Project Manager and QA team
- Maintain rollback procedures and incident response runbooks
- Enforce security and compliance standards in the delivery pipeline

### Goals
- Achieve high deployment frequency with low failure rate
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) when incidents occur
- Keep infrastructure secure, cost-efficient, and scalable

### Typical Communication
- Release scheduling meetings with PM and QA
- Incident postmortems and runbook updates
- Infrastructure updates in the project board and release notes

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers translate user needs and business goals into intuitive, accessible product experiences. They conduct research, create prototypes, and collaborate with Product Managers and Developers throughout the build process.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research, interviews, and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows, information architecture, and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with Product Managers to align designs with product goals
- Work with Developers to ensure faithful, accessible implementation
- Maintain and evolve the design system and style guidelines

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and delightful
- Ground design decisions in user research and validated assumptions
- Reduce user friction and support task completion

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and developers
- Usability test readouts and research findings
- Design handoff documentation and annotated specs

---

## Security Engineers

### Role Summary
Security Engineers identify and mitigate security risks across the product and infrastructure. They embed security practices into the development lifecycle and ensure the organization meets compliance requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct threat modeling, security reviews, and penetration testing
- Review code and infrastructure changes for security vulnerabilities
- Define and enforce secure coding standards and policies
- Manage vulnerability scanning tooling and triage findings
- Collaborate with DevOps on security controls in CI/CD pipelines
- Lead incident response for security events and coordinate remediation

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Build a security-first culture within the engineering organization
- Maintain compliance with relevant regulatory and industry standards

### Typical Communication
- Security review sessions during planning and design phases
- Vulnerability reports and risk registers with prioritized findings
- Post-incident security retrospectives and remediation tracking

---

## Technical Leads

### Role Summary
Technical Leads guide the architectural vision and technical quality of a project. They mentor developers, make key design decisions, and bridge communication between engineering and product/project management.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the technical architecture and design principles
- Review and approve significant architectural decisions (ADRs)
- Mentor and support developers through code reviews and pairing
- Identify and manage technical debt and risk
- Collaborate with Project Managers on capacity planning and estimation
- Ensure the team adheres to coding standards and quality practices

### Goals
- Deliver a maintainable, scalable, and observable technical solution
- Grow the technical capability of the team
- Keep technical risk visible and manageable

### Typical Communication
- Architecture and design review sessions
- Code reviews and technical design documentation
- Escalation point for complex technical blockers or trade-off decisions
